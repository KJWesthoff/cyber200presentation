Who Defines ISO 27001

A consensus-based, voluntary international standards group.

  • ISO and IEC Joint Technical Committee (JTC 1) for information technology (meets every 6-12 months [3])
    • Advisory groups (experts)
    • Subcommittees (develop standards) (SC 27)
    • Working groups (task forces)

Conclusion

Standards are critically needed in cybersecurity (as they are in many other applications), it is shocking how much is left unregulated in the hands of business (with the whole context thereof (short sighted ness)(only answering ot shareholders) etc..) With its high level of abstraction, the ISO/IEC 27001 standard provides a methodology covering the cybersecurity scope and ensuring alignment of a business. The standard does not address specific subjects, these are to be addressed by domain specific rule works, with which the ISO 27001 works well in parallel. Even though the ISO 27001 does not address specific details, having it in place will facilitate achieving compliance with other standards, this makes it well suited for the future.
