Standards in context

A bit on what standards are and what they do in other industries

Shipping

Special case (with some odd similarities to the internet):
Similar
  • Handles goods in transit
  • Travels through international waters
  • Sometimes, malicious content is transported in packages
Different
  • B2B Only
  • Large Single Assets
  • Infrastructure and Cargo
  • Highly Regulated

Standards in Shipping

The Electrical Grid

Similar
  • Both Consumer and Business
  • Critical Infrastructure
  • Highly Privatized
Different
  • Single, Traded Commodity
  • Enclosed Networks, with differences between countries
  • One Direction (For now..Future Smart Grids)

Standards in the Electrical Grid

What Standards Do

  • Mitigate Liability
  • Ensure alignment.
  • Enable new tech to market

Standards often feel wired or not the most rational, as they a often "solidified over time rather than cast in a mold"

ISO 27001 Dive-in

  • Not very long (19 pages)
  • Very high level
  • Intended for many applications, big and small
  • Information Security Management System (ISMS)
    • List of 'controls'

How

  • Risk Assessment
  • Define Scope of ISMS
    • Which parts of your business is covered
  • Implement Controls
    • Explain which apply and why
    • Risk Mitigation plan

Certification

  • Audit
    • By a Certification Body
      • Which is Accredited (by ANSI in the US)
